Boundaries.

Commas, semicolons, colons, dashes. Each mark has one job. Learn the jobs and never miss a Boundaries question again.

The Golden Rule precise & concise.
The Strategy
Boundaries

Punctuation has one job each.

The strat

  1. Read the parts on either side of the blank
  2. Are they complete sentences? (Subject + verb + a full thought)
  3. If yes → semicolon, period, or comma + conjunction
  4. If no → comma (subordinate clause) or no punctuation (tight phrase)

Punctuation cheat sheet

,
Comma — short pauses, lists, after intro clauses, around optional info
;
Semicolon — joins two complete sentences with a connection
:
Colon — introduces a list, explanation, or description (needs complete sentence before it)
—
Dash — like a colon or commas, more emphatic
.
Period — ends a complete sentence

The 3 deadly traps

  • Comma splice — comma alone between two complete sentences
  • Run-on — no punctuation between two complete sentences
  • Unnecessary commas around essential information (restrictive appositives)

Quick test

Ask of every blank: can each side stand alone as a sentence? If yes → you need real separation (semicolon, period, or comma + FANBOYS). If no → just a comma (or nothing).
