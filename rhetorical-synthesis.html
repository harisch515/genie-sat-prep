The Strategy
Rhetorical Synthesis

The goal is the whole question.

The strat

  1. Read the GOAL in the question stem — twice
  2. Underline the goal verb (introduce, contrast, specify, emphasize)
  3. Eliminate any choice that doesn't do that exact thing — even if it's true
  4. Pick the most precise match

Goal verb decoder

specify the reason X was created
Why — answer the why ("to ___")
provide an overview
All — most COMPLETE choice
emphasize a similarity
Both+Same — both items + shared trait
explain an advantage
Compare — better/less/more than
specify how X enables Y
How — describe the mechanism
place ___ in historical context
Time — before / during / after
audience already familiar with X
Skip-X — don't define X

The #1 trap: true but wrong

Most wrong choices are factually true. They use real facts from the notes. They sound informed. They just don't accomplish the SPECIFIC goal asked. Fix: ask of every "good-sounding" choice — does this actually do what the verb said?

Trap answers

  • True statement that doesn't match the GOAL
  • Mentions only ONE item when goal asks about both
  • Background info instead of the asked-for content
