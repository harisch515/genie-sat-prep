Command of Evidence.

Textual evidence and chart-reading. Find the choice that directly proves the claim — no more, no less. True isn't enough; it has to match.

The Golden Rule precise & concise.
The Strategy
Command of Evidence

Match the claim. Pick the proof.

The strat

  1. Read the CLAIM first — it's usually bolded or italicized
  2. Underline the key verbs in the claim (what does it say happens?)
  3. For text: find the choice that DIRECTLY proves the claim
  4. For charts: find the data point that DIRECTLY matches the claim

Two flavors

TEXTUAL
Choices are findings/quotes. Pick the one that proves the EXACT claim — not "related to" or "similar to."
QUANTITATIVE
A chart + a claim. Read the claim, find the choice that describes data that matches it. Skip "true but irrelevant" descriptions.

The killer trap: true but irrelevant

Most wrong choices are true. They use real facts or real data from the chart. They just don't prove the SPECIFIC claim. Fix: ask of every "good-sounding" choice — does this actually prove what the claim says?

Trap answers

  • True but doesn't prove the claim — relevant topic, wrong specifics
  • From the wrong time — chart data from before/after the relevant period
  • Tangential comparison — comparing to something the claim didn't mention
  • Contradicts the claim — sometimes hidden behind a familiar-sounding statement
